3760. Denis Duquet

Denis Duquet est l'ancêtre de toutes nos familles Duquet et Duquette devieille souche. On ignore son origine, ainsi que le nom de ses parents.Il est né vers 1612.

Vers l'âge de 26 ans, le 13 mai 1638, il épouse Catherine Gauthier, néevers 1625, originaire de la paroisse St-Étienne du Mont, à Paris, France,fille de Philippe Gauthier et de Marie Pichon. Ce mariage est le seizièmeinscrit dans les registres de Québec.

Denis Duquet et Catherine Gauthier deviennent les parents de onzeenfants, dont six fils.

Le 3 novembre 1672, l'intendant Talon concède une seigneurie à l'ancêtreDenis Duquet, située sur la rive sud du fleuve, près de celle de Tilly.

L'ancêtre Denis Duquet décède le 26 novembre 1675, à Lauzon, âgé de 70ans, inhumé le lendemain à Québec.

CRAMPON, Catherine

Catherine est née en 1642 à Saint-Sulpice-de-Paris, France. Ses parentsétaient Jean Crampon et Helène Richard.

Catherine aurait eu un premier mariage avec Louis Pépin. Widowed by LouisPepin before leaving France.

The Filles à Marier were women that came to Canada, or New France, in theyears 1634-1662. The first few years, girls arrived in numbers fewerthan 3. They were sponsored by individuals that had to account for theirmorality and honesty. In later years, they began to come in groups, orgreater numbers per year, with 37 being the most in one year - 1662. Fewof the women who came to New France had relatives living there. Mostcame with to escape life in France - poverty, arranged marriages. Unlikethe Filles du Roi, they were not recruited, were not given a dowry.However, they had the freedom to marry whom they chose, make a life oftheir own. In the nearly 30 year period that an effort was made to sendmariagable girls to New France.
