2037221491. Pétronille de Comminges

Comtesse de Bigorre. Vicomtesse de Marsan.

died after 3 November 1251 (date will)

Petronille, Countess of Bigorre (d. 1251) [ES III:405] married five times. By her third husband, Guy de Montfort-l'Amaury (brother of Simon, Earl of Leicester and son-in-law of King John), Petronille was the grandmother of Eschivat III de Chabannais [ES III:642 and XIV:109]. Her fifth husband was Boson de Mastas, seigneur de Cognac (d. 1251).
Petronille's grandmother was a daughter of Alphonse Jourdain, Count of Toulouse (d. 1148) [ES III:764]. He was a first cousin of Philippa, grandmother of Eleanor of Aquitaine. So Eschivat III was a fifth cousin of Henry III by this line.

Boson de Mastas, and his genealogical connections are well-known. He became comte de Bigorre when he married (as her fourth husband) Petronille de Comminges. On her death, in 1251, his continuation was disputed by Petronille's heiress, Alix de Montfort (daughter of her second husband, Gui de Montfort [+1220]), defending the rights of her son. Alix was married to Jourdain de Chabannais, and their son was Eskivat de Chabannais, who became comte de Bigorre in 1251 and lasted until 1283. Eskivat also held Armagnac from 1249 to 1256.
